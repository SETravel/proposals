Next: Meet the Ships
Two ships sail short cruises from Galveston this summer — Carnival Breeze and Royal Caribbean's Mariner of the Seas. Both are great for a mom-and-son graduation trip. Here's a quick look at each.
Fun Ship · Waterslide · Ropes Course
Carnival Breeze
Dream Class · 3,690 Guests · Galveston Terminal 25

Carnival Breeze is Carnival's signature "Fun Ship" sailing year-round from Galveston. She features the 320-foot Twister Waterslide, the SkyRide suspended bike course, Guy's Burger Joint, a full spa, and dedicated youth programs for teens. Sails alternating 4-night (Cozumel only) and 5-night (Cozumel + Progreso) itineraries — the most budget-friendly option.

FlowRider · Ice Rink · Escape Room
Mariner of the Seas
Voyager Class · 3,300 Guests · Galveston Terminal 10

Royal Caribbean's Mariner of the Seas is loaded with unique activities — the FlowRider surf simulator, an ice-skating rink, glow-in-the-dark laser tag, a trampoline park, and an escape room. She sails alternating 4-night (Cozumel only) and 5-night (Costa Maya + Cozumel) itineraries. Slightly higher price point, but more onboard activities for Landyn.

Next: Where You'll Visit
Every sailing visits Cozumel — it's the anchor port for all Galveston short cruises. The difference is the second stop: Carnival adds Progreso (gateway to Mérida and the Mayan ruins), while Royal Caribbean adds Costa Maya (beach clubs and the Great Maya Reef). Both are wonderful — here's what makes each special.
Every Sailing

Cozumel, Mexico

The crown jewel of Western Caribbean cruising. Crystal-clear water, world-class snorkeling at Palancar Reef, and easy access to the ancient Mayan ruins at Tulum on the mainland. Downtown San Miguel has great shopping and authentic Mexican food. A perfect mix of adventure and relaxation.

Snorkeling & Diving Mayan Ruins (Tulum) Beach Clubs Shopping · San Miguel Excursions: ~$45–120/pp
Royal Caribbean Sailings Only

Costa Maya, Mexico

A quieter, more relaxed port with a stunning beach club right at the cruise terminal. The turquoise Bacalar Lagoon — known as the "Lake of Seven Colors" — is a popular excursion. Great for swimming, kayaking, and soaking up the sun in a less crowded setting than Cozumel.

Bacalar Lagoon Great Maya Reef Beach Club at Port Chacchoben Ruins Excursions: ~$40–100/pp
Carnival Sailings Only

Progreso (Mérida), Mexico

The gateway to the Yucatán's cultural capital, Mérida. The big draw here is the ancient Mayan ruins of Uxmal — less crowded than Chichén Itzá but equally impressive. Swim in a cenote (natural limestone sinkhole), explore colonial architecture in Mérida, or relax at the long, shallow beach.

Uxmal Mayan Ruins Cenote Swimming Colonial Mérida Shallow Beach Excursions: ~$40–110/pp
