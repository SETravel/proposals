The drive from Nashville to Memphis is about 3 hours on I-40 West — a scenic, straightforward interstate drive. We recommend leaving Nashville around 8:30 AM Sunday morning, which gets you to Memphis by 11:30 AM with time for lunch before your 1:30 PM Graceland tour.

This is the simplest option — no changes needed. After Graceland Sunday, overnight in Memphis, fly home Monday. The rental car is one-way Nashville→Memphis, which typically adds a $50–150 drop-off fee on top of the base rental rate. Estimated rental car cost: ~$280–400 total for 4 days one-way.

Route B: Drive Back to Nashville, Fly Home from BNA

Requires rebooking return flights. After Graceland Sunday, overnight in Memphis, then drive ~3 hours back to Nashville Monday morning. Drop rental car at BNA and fly home.

Potential savings: A round-trip Nashville rental (no one-way fee) runs about ~$130–200 for 4 days — saving $100–200 on the car. However, rebooking one-way return flights BNA→DFW→CLL for 2 travelers on June 29 would likely cost ~$180–250 per person (based on current AA pricing of ~$180+ one-way BNA→CLL via DFW). That's $360–500 for two new one-way tickets.

The math: You'd save ~$100–200 on the car but likely spend $360–500 on new flights, plus lose the value of the already-booked Memphis return legs. The existing MEM flights are non-changeable Main Cabin fares — any changes would be at the fare difference.

Additional consideration: Monday morning would be a 3-hour drive + airport time, making for a very long travel day for an 80-year-old. The current plan (Memphis→DFW→CLL) is much easier on Louise.

Our recommendation: Stick with Route A (fly from Memphis). The one-way drop fee is a small price for convenience, comfort, and keeping the existing flights intact.
