DESTINATIONS

Two Caribbean Ports

Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula — different vibes, two incredible days.
DAY 3 · MONDAY · JUNE 1
Puerto Costa Maya
Quintana Roo, Mexico

Off the Beaten Path

Where the jungle meets the reef.

Costa Maya sits 30 miles south of Cancun and feels a world away from it. The cruise port is a self-contained village with shops, restaurants, swimming pools, and that Mayan-pyramid-style waterslide complex you can see from the ship.

Beyond the port gates: the fishing village of Mahahual, the Great Maya Reef, and ancient ruins still half-swallowed by jungle. A slower, more authentic stop — perfect for either a chill beach-club day or an active adventure.

DAY 4 · TUESDAY · JUNE 2
Cozumel
Quintana Roo, Mexico

Crown Jewel of the Caribbean

Some of the clearest water you'll ever swim in.

Cozumel sits on the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef, the second-largest reef system in the world. Jacques Cousteau called it the best diving destination on the planet back in 1961, and it has stayed in the conversation ever since. Visibility regularly exceeds 100 feet.

Beyond the water, you've got beach clubs with all-day food and drinks, the Mayan ruins at San Gervasio, the buzzy waterfront town of San Miguel, and ATV/Jeep tours that loop the wilder eastern side of the island.

A practical tip from us: For Cozumel especially, we strongly recommend pre-booking at least one excursion. Beach clubs sell out for cruise days weeks in advance, and the cruise ship can wait for you if you book through Royal Caribbean's official excursions. Independent operators are ~30% cheaper but the responsibility for getting back on time is on you. We can book either route — just say the word.
