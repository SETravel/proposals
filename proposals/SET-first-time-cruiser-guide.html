Your First Royal Caribbean Cruise

The essentials — distilled. Everything you actually need to know before you sail.

Royal Caribbean publishes dozens of pre-cruise FAQ pages. This is the short version — the twelve things that matter for a first-time cruiser, plus a clean timeline of what happens when. Anything more specific, just call us.
The Countdown

Your cruise timeline

At Booking
Deposit secures your room Some fares require a non-refundable deposit — confirm with us which fare type you're on. Your room category is locked in now; upgrades onboard are very rare.
75–120 days out
Final payment is due Royal's deadlines: 75 days before sailing for 1–4 night cruises, 90 days for 5–14 night cruises, 120 days for 15+ night cruises. We'll remind you well before.
45 days out
Online check-in opens Use the Royal Caribbean app to upload your passport photo, take a security selfie, and pick your boarding arrival window. Don't skip this — it determines what time you board.
30 days out
Pre-buy beverage & dining packages Pre-cruise prices are typically lower than onboard. Beverage packages can be purchased online up to 72 hours before sailing.
A few days out
Print luggage tags & boarding pass Royal's eDocs include personalized luggage tags with your ship, sail date, stateroom, and deck. Generic tags are also available at the pier if you'd rather grab one there.
Sail Day
Arrive within your window Show up during the arrival time you picked — not before, not after. Early arrivals wait; late arrivals risk missing the ship.
The Twelve Essentials

What every first-time cruiser should know

01
Identification
Bring a passport
A passport isn't always required, but it's strongly recommended for every sailing. A certified U.S. birth certificate plus government photo ID can work for closed-loop Caribbean cruises — but a passport is non-negotiable peace of mind.
02
Final Payment
Mark your calendar
75 days for 1–4 night cruises · 90 days for 5–14 night · 120 days for 15+ night. Miss it and your booking can be cancelled.
03
Gratuities
$21 per person, per day
Standard rooms: $21/day per guest. Suites: $23.00/day per guest. Charged automatically to your SeaPass account daily — or you can prepay at booking and forget about it.
04
Dining
Traditional or My Time
Traditional: assigned table, early or late seating. My Time: dine when you want, reservations recommended. You pick at booking.
05
Alcohol Policy
Wine yes, liquor no
Each guest 21+ may bring two 750ml bottles of wine or champagne in carry-on at embarkation. Beer and hard liquor are not permitted. Anything seized is returned at the end of the voyage.
06
Beverage Packages
Buy ahead, save more
Pre-cruise prices typically beat onboard rates. Packages are per person and cannot be shared — only one drink per order. Available online up to 72 hours before sailing.
07
Shore Excursions
Book through Royal
Booking with Royal gets you priority debarkation at port — first off the ship — and a guarantee the ship will wait if your excursion runs late. Available pre-cruise via My Royal Cruise.
08
Best Price Guarantee
Watch for price drops
Find a lower published rate on the same cruise, date, and room type? Royal will match it. Tell us — we'll handle the request on your behalf.
09
Dress Code
Mostly casual
Day-to-day: resort casual — jeans, polos, sundresses. Evenings include 1–2 "smart casual" or formal nights depending on cruise length. No shorts or swimwear in the Main Dining Room at dinner.
10
Arrival Time
Stick to your slot
You'll select an arrival window during app check-in. Arrive within it. Early = you wait outside. Late = you may not board. The window is on your mobile boarding pass.
11
Travel Protection
Strongly recommended
Cruises are large, non-refundable purchases with strict cancellation windows. Royal's program covers cancellation, medical, baggage, and emergency evacuation. We'll walk you through it.
12
Onboard Account
Your SeaPass card
Your room key, ID, and onboard charge card all in one. Cash isn't used onboard — every purchase posts to your SeaPass account, which settles to your credit card at the end of the cruise.
The One That Trips People Up

What ID do I need?

★ Strongly Recommended

Valid Passport

Works for every itinerary, every port, every contingency. Must be valid for at least six months past your return date. If you ever need to fly home from a foreign port mid-trip, this is the only document that gets you on the plane.

Acceptable for U.S. Closed-Loop

Birth Certificate + Photo ID

U.S. citizens on cruises that begin and end at the same U.S. port can sail with a state-certified birth certificate (with raised seal) plus a government-issued photo ID. Doesn't work if your itinerary touches certain destinations — always confirm with us.
