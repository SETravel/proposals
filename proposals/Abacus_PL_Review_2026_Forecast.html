01 — Where We've Been

The 2024 → 2025 Story

We doubled gross revenue and swung from a $1.3M loss to a $413K profit. The growth was concentrated in a few channels — and that creates both a challenge and an opportunity heading into 2026.

In 2024, Abacus Brands brought in $6.5M in gross product sales against an operating expense base of $3.5M, posting a net loss of $1.27M. That loss reflected the cost of building the infrastructure — payroll, trade shows, paid advertising — needed to support a product line that was still finding its channels.

2025 was the breakout. Gross product sales more than doubled to $13.8M, driven by two major wins: Costco contributed $3M (from zero) and Amazon/Online grew 29% to $4.3M. Walmart in-store went from $540 to $579K. Barnes & Noble grew 34% to $212K. Net profit swung to +$413K.

The Costco program is on pause for 2026 — not a lost relationship, but a reset year while we develop the product mix that brings them back to the table. That $3M gap is the central challenge of this forecast, and it's also the catalyst for the diversification work that makes 2027 and beyond stronger.

Concentration → Opportunity

Amazon + Costco made up 53% of 2025 gross revenue. With the Costco program on pause, 2026 is an opportunity to build the diversified channel base that reduces reliance on any single account. Every channel outside of Costco is forecast to grow — the question is how much, and how fast we can activate new accounts.

What Went Right

Walmart in-store: $540 → $579K. From a rounding error to a real account. +30% SKU expansion planned for 2026 validates the relationship.

Walmart.com: $6.6K → $381K. The marketplace presence proved out at scale — 57x growth.

Nordstrom: $58K → $122K. 110% YoY growth in a premium channel. Gift items are working.

Specialty channel: grew to ~299 stores. Net new doors added across indie toy, museum, and gift retail. Average revenue per store held at ~$1,150.

Net profit swing: ($1.27M) → +$413K. A $1.68M improvement. Revenue doubled while opex grew only 23% — the operating leverage is real.

Areas to Watch

Costco program on pause. A strong partnership that will likely resume once we have the right product mix (Pixicade Star Wars, Barbie, Radical Games are all strong candidates). For now, $3M to replace in 2026.

Deductions were elevated in 2025. Returns, discounts, and chargebacks totaled 25.9% of gross — driven primarily by Costco's channel dynamics. Without that volume in the mix, we expect a meaningful improvement.

Some accounts may have gone quiet. We don't have full visibility into account-level data from Odoo, so the exact status of some smaller accounts needs to be verified as we refine the forecast.

Royalties growing with licensed products. Increased from $28K to $187K as NatGeo, ESPN, and other licensed lines scaled. This is a feature, not a bug — but it does impact per-unit margin.

Large pipeline, early-stage. 74 prospect accounts and 31 pipeline accounts represent significant future opportunity. Converting even a fraction changes the picture meaningfully.

Key Takeaway

At base case, 2026 is a reset year. The Costco pause creates a revenue gap while the cost structure that was built for scale remains in place. The opportunity: every non-Costco channel is growing, the product pipeline is the strongest it's ever been, and the new tier framework gives us a clear map of where incremental revenue lives. The path to profitability runs through new account activation and the Fall product launches.

Deductions: Returning to Baseline

In 2024, total revenue deductions (chargebacks, discounts, returns) ran at 13.1% of gross. In 2025, that spiked to 25.9% — driven overwhelmingly by Costco's channel dynamics (high club-channel return rates, volume discounts, and chargeback patterns). With the Costco program paused, we're using the 2024 rate as our starting point (13.1%), with a modest ~0.4% uplift to account for Walmart in-store now being a meaningful part of the mix. Big box retail carries slightly higher deduction rates than DTC or specialty. That gives us a 2026 estimate of ~13.5% — a significant improvement from 2025, and grounded in what we actually experienced pre-Costco.

Cost of Revenue: Holding the Line

Cost of revenue dropped from 53.4% of net in 2024 to 40.0% in 2025 — that improvement reflected real operational efficiencies in how the sales force was deployed, fulfillment operations were streamlined, and account management was professionalized. For 2026, we're holding COR flat at the 2025 dollar amount ($5.5M). The goal is to maintain the efficiencies gained, not let them erode as the revenue mix shifts. As a percentage of a smaller revenue base, COR will look higher — but the absolute dollar discipline is what matters. This is a realistic hold, not an optimistic cut.

06 — New Division

Radical Games: Opening New Doors

We launched an entirely new division in 2026. Radical Games is fun-first: party games, novelty, collectibles, and gifts. This isn't an extension of the STEM/educational catalog — it's a new aperture into channels and retailers Abacus has never sold to before.

Until now, Abacus Brands has been an educational toy and VR/AR company. That positions us well in specialty, book chains, and STEM-focused retail — but it limits the doors we can walk through. Radical Games changes that. Party games, travel games, collectible plush, and puzzles are welcome at retailers that would never carry a VR Science Kit: game stores, gift shops, convenience, airport retail, college bookstores, lifestyle chains, dollar/value retailers, and mass-market game aisles.

The early traction is real: 3 games already placed at Target for Fall 2026 — Lick My Nachos with 2 exclusive SKUs through January 2027 (Froggy Frenzy and Trick Shot Trivia). Target placement validates the line for every other buyer conversation.

08 — The Path Forward

What Needs to Be True for 2026–2027

The priorities, opportunities, and open questions that shape the next 18 months.

The Revenue Equation

At base case, domestic gross is $9.75M. To break even, we need roughly $12.5M in gross (adding ~$2.75M above base). To match 2025's profitability, we need closer to $13.5M. To hit the $15M target, we need the product launches to deliver and the pipeline to convert. It's a big number — but the pieces are in front of us.

Must-Win Priorities

1. Protect Amazon. At $5.6M forecast, it's 58% of base. The +30% target is achievable with Radical Games catalog expansion driving incremental volume.

2. Activate Walmart (in-store) aggressively. 4,606 doors × $2,500/door = $11.5M potential. We're at $579K. The quarterly sticker refresh + Pixicade SKU addition is the right strategy to build per-door penetration.

3. Re-engage Sam's Club. 601 doors at warehouse club economics, with a prior $281K track record. The most natural near-term replacement for Costco volume.

4. Land Kohls for Fall. 1,153 doors, Pixicade angle. Pipeline status with Jodie connection. $576K potential.

5. Convert AAFES. 150 military exchange doors. Barbie + lower retails. LA meeting is the key moment.

Things Worth Discussing

1. Pipeline conversion rate. 105 accounts at Prospect/Pipeline status with ~$265K total forecast. Setting a realistic Q3 conversion target would help the team focus effort on the highest-probability wins.

2. Fall launch sell-in timeline. Star Wars, Barbie, Globe Alive all need retail placement secured by summer. Understanding the timeline helps sales plan around it.

3. Cost structure at lower volume. At $4.66M forecast, opex is built for scale. Worth understanding which line items flex if revenue comes in closer to conservative — and which are locked in regardless.

4. Costco re-engagement path. Pixicade Star Wars and Barbie are both strong candidates to bring back to the table. Worth mapping the timeline so we're ready when the conversation opens.

5. International potential. Innovation First (AU) is growing fast, with several new 2025 partners in Asia and Europe. There may be more upside here with the right product mix and support from Jay's network.

Areas to Align On — Monday

Paid ad spend. Currently forecast at $950K. There's been discussion about increasing this to drive DTC — worth reviewing the ROI model together to see if the numbers support it.

Team capacity. Payroll at +10% assumes the current team. As we push into hardware, F&S, and Radical Games channels, it's worth assessing whether the sales team has the bandwidth or if we'll need to add capacity.

Fall launch modeling. Right now the base forecast doesn't assume full launch success for Star Wars, Barbie, or Globe Alive. The team should align on how much launch revenue to build into the working plan vs. keeping as upside.

Tier review. The rated $/door values in the tier framework are a starting point. Would be helpful to get input from the team on whether any channels are over- or under-estimated.

$15M scenario. We should build out the full $15M lane in the forecast sheet so the team can track against it quarterly.

09 — Looking Ahead

The 2027 Horizon

2026 is the reset year. 2027 is when the portfolio strategy compounds — if we execute now.

The product catalog is getting broader and more licensable. Pixicade Star Wars and Barbie — if they prove at retail in Fall 2026 — create a repeatable playbook for licensed versions that open mass-market doors. NatGeo Globe Alive, at $59.99 MSRP, positions us in the premium gift/educational segment where margins are strongest.

The tier analysis shows $38.2M in total domestic revenue potential across 150 accounts. In 2025 we captured roughly $10.2M of that. Even moving from ~27% capture to 35% capture would put us at $13.4M domestic. The infrastructure — sales team, rep network, trade shows, DTC — is already built and funded.

The critical 2027 milestones:

Full-year revenue from Fall 2026 launches. Star Wars, Barbie, Globe Alive will have a full year of retail data. Strong sell-through rates turn into 2027 reorders and expanded placement.

Hardware and Farm & Supply channel activation. 63 Tier D accounts with $3.4M combined potential. Mid-States Distributing is the proof-of-concept — member store performance unlocks the rest of the network.

Radical Games as a channel multiplier. The party games and collectible plush line (Hoodeez, Froggy Frenzy, Lick My Nachos, Trick Shot Trivia, Tug of Draw) opens doors at retailers that don't carry STEM/educational — Five Below, Spencer's, Party City, college bookstores, convenience, dollar/value, and lifestyle chains. With Target placement already secured for Fall 2026, 2027 sell-through data unlocks the next wave of retail conversations. Every new door for Radical Games is a door where the Abacus catalog can follow.

Costco re-engagement. With proven retail data on Star Wars, Barbie, and Globe Alive, the conversation with Costco becomes about which products to bring back, not whether to come back. This is a 2027 revenue event we should be planning for now.

The Case for Optimism

Abacus went from a $1.3M loss to profitability in one year by finding the right channels and executing. The team, infrastructure, and product pipeline are all materially stronger going into 2026 than they were going into 2025. The Costco pause is real — but it also forces the diversification that makes the business more resilient long-term. If even half of the 2026 product launches land, and we convert a fraction of the pipeline, 2027 starts to look like the year the portfolio model proves out — and the year Costco comes back to the table.
