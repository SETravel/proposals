Day-by-Day

Complete Itinerary

7 nights at sea through Alaska's Inside Passage, followed by a 3-night land adventure from Seward through Talkeetna and Denali to Anchorage. Every port day has excursion options — see the next section for details.

2
Vancouver, BC — Pre-Night
Fly in July 2 · Overnight
Arrive Vancouver, check into waterfront hotel near Canada Place terminal. Stanley Park, Gastown, and Coal Harbour are steps away. Ship departs tomorrow at 5:00 PM.
1
Vancouver, BC — Embarkation
July 3 · Departs 5:00 PM
Board Celebrity Summit at Canada Place. All ship amenities open immediately — spa, restaurants, pool deck, Sunset Bar. Sail north into the Inside Passage as evening falls.
2
Inside Passage — Scenic Cruising
July 4 · Full Sea Day
Sail Alaska's inner waterways — a labyrinth of fjords, bays, and green islands teeming with orca, mountain goats, bald eagles, and puffins. Extended daylight hours make for excellent wildlife photography all day.
3
Ketchikan, Alaska
July 5 · Docked 7:00 AM – 3:00 PM
The "Salmon Capital of the World" and ancestral home of the Tlingit people. Downtown is walkable from the ship — totem poles, Creek Street boardwalk, and fresh Dungeness crab. Full excursion menu available (see below).
4
Sitka, Alaska
July 6 · Docked 7:00 AM – 3:30 PM
Former Russian capital until 1867 — St. Michael's Orthodox Cathedral still stands. Nestled on the Gulf of Alaska coast with humpback whale watching from the promenade, coastal brown bears in Tongass National Forest, and the oldest museum in Alaska.
5
Juneau, Alaska
July 7 · Tendered 7:00 AM – 9:30 PM
Alaska's capital — the longest port day of the trip at over 14 hours ashore. Home to Mendenhall Glacier, the Mt. Roberts Tram, and helicopter glacier dog sledding tours. A full day to explore.
6
Icy Strait Point, Alaska
July 8 · Tendered 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM
The only Native-owned and operated port in Alaska — owned by the Hoonah Tlingit community. Prime humpback whale and orca habitat. Features North America's longest ZipRider, ATV adventures, and the world's largest brown bear population per square mile nearby.
7
Hubbard Glacier — Scenic Cruising
July 9 · 6:00 AM – 10:00 AM
The longest tidewater glacier in the world — 76 miles long, 7 miles wide, rising 30 stories above sea level. The ship enters Disenchantment Bay for close-up views of active calving. An onboard naturalist narrates as ice thunders into the sea. Whales, harbor seals, and otters hunt in the icy waters below.
8
Seward, Alaska — Disembark & Land Tour Begins
July 10 · Arrives 6:00 AM
Arrive in Seward at the foot of the Kenai Mountains. Disembark, meet your Tour Director, and begin the 3-night land adventure. First stop: Seward Harbor — smoked salmon tasting and Alaska's rich fishing history.
L1
Land Tour Day 1
Seward → Anchorage → Talkeetna
July 10 · Overnight: Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge
Seward Harbor tour + smoked salmon tasting. Motor coach north to Anchorage — visit Alaska Native Heritage Center, sample Akutaq (Eskimo Ice Cream). Continue to Talkeetna for beer tasting at local brewery. Evening free to explore Talkeetna's backcountry Main Street.
L2
Land Tour Day 2
Talkeetna → Wilderness Express Rail → Denali
July 11 · Overnight: Denali Park Village
Morning free in Talkeetna (optional flightseeing available). Board the Wilderness Express glass-domed railcar for a scenic ride to Denali. Afternoon free at Denali Park Village on the Nenana River — trails or optional white water rafting.

Denali Natural History Tour into the national park — moose, caribou, bears, and Dall sheep. Motor coach to Anchorage. Evening free: Tony Knowles Coastal Trail, downtown dining, late-evening sun (Alaska summer stays light until midnight).

Agent's Note on Excursion Budget

For 2 guests doing 1 major excursion per port across Ketchikan, Sitka, Juneau, and Icy Strait — budget approximately $1,200–$2,000 total (both guests combined). Post-Cruise · 3 Nights

The Alaska Land Adventure

This is what separates a cruisetour from a standard cruise. The 3-night land package begins the moment the ship docks in Seward and takes you deep into Alaska's interior — salmon, rail, Denali, and Anchorage — before flying home. It's a complete mini-trip in its own right.

Land Tour Day 1 · July 10
Seward → Anchorage → Talkeetna

Meet your dedicated Tour Director at the Seward dock. Begin with a guided stroll of Seward Harbor, learning Alaska's rich fishing history and sampling locally produced smoked salmon — the "Tastes of Alaska" moment the tour is named for.

Deluxe motor coach north through the Kenai Peninsula to Anchorage, passing the dramatic Turnagain Arm. Stop at the Alaska Native Heritage Center to learn about indigenous cultures and sample Akutaq (traditional Eskimo Ice Cream).

Continue north to Talkeetna for a beer tasting at a local brewery. Evening free to wander Talkeetna's quirky Main Street — backcountry cafés, brew pubs, and converted Airstream trailers serving Alaskan fare under the midnight sun.

🏨
Overnight: Talkeetna Alaskan Lodge — sits above the Susitna River valley with stunning views of Denali (weather permitting). A beloved Alaska lodge property.
➕
Optional upgrade: Talkeetna flightseeing tour over Denali and the Alaska Range (~$350–$450 pp, book in advance — popular in July)
Land Tour Day 2 · July 11
Talkeetna → Wilderness Express → Denali

Morning free in Talkeetna — stroll the trails or book the optional flightseeing tour over the Alaska Range.

Then board the Wilderness Express — Celebrity's signature glass-domed railcar. The train climbs through boreal forest and alpine tundra with floor-to-ceiling windows on all sides. Narrated journey. Dining available on board. This is the rail ride included in the competitor package.

Arrive at Denali — the 6-million-acre national park and preserve that's larger than Massachusetts. Afternoon and evening free to hike the river trails around the lodge or book an optional Nenana River rafting excursion.

🏨
Overnight: Denali Park Village — set on the banks of the Nenana River, with direct access to park trails and the wilderness atmosphere of interior Alaska.
➕
Optional upgrade: Nenana River white water rafting (~$65–$95 pp) or gentle float trip (~$45–$65 pp)
Land Tour Day 3 · July 12
Denali Natural History Tour → Anchorage

Morning on the Denali Natural History Tour — a narrated bus journey into the park's restricted interior. Guided wildlife search across taiga forests and rolling tundra: moose, caribou, Dall sheep, grizzly bears, and wolves. On a clear day, views of Denali (20,310 ft) fill the entire horizon.

Afternoon motor coach south to Anchorage. Arrive by 5:00 PM with the evening free — Tony Knowles Coastal Trail along Cook Inlet, excellent restaurants downtown (Orso, Glacier Brewhouse, Humpy's Great Alaskan Alehouse), and Alaska's summer light lasting until midnight.

🏨
Overnight: Anchorage Marriott Downtown — the anchor hotel of the tour, well-positioned for the airport transfer in the morning.
