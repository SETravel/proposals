BARCELONA ITINERARY — MAY 25–28
Day 1May 25
Arrival & The Gothic Quarter
Arrive at BCN after the overnight flight and check in to your hotel. Shake off the journey with a slow afternoon stroll through the Barri Gòtic — cobblestone lanes, Roman ruins just beneath the surface, and the magnificent Barcelona Cathedral. Let the evening unfold naturally with a glass of cava and tapas on a sidewalk terrace.
Day 2May 26
Gaudí Day & Dinner at Lasarte
Begin with a pre-booked morning visit to the Sagrada Família — its soaring interior of kaleidoscopic light is unlike anything else on Earth. Afternoon at Casa Batlló or La Pedrera, both within strolling distance on Passeig de Gràcia. That evening, dinner at Lasarte (3 Michelin stars, Monument Hotel) — Chef Martín Berasategui's Barcelona crown jewel, widely considered one of Spain's finest tables.
Day 3May 27
Barceloneta, Picasso & Last Evening
Morning at the Picasso Museum in El Born, then walk to Barceloneta Beach for a long Mediterranean lunch at a seafront chiringuito. The afternoon is yours — Montjuïc by cable car, the colorful Boqueria market, or simply a spa treatment back at the hotel. Final evening tapas crawl through El Born neighborhood, where the city's most vibrant bars and wine bars line every corner.
Day 4May 28
Embarkation — Norwegian Aura
A relaxed morning checkout, then transfer to the Port of Barcelona (approximately 20 minutes) to embark the brand-new Norwegian Aura for her inaugural transatlantic crossing to Miami. Raise a glass in The Haven Bar as you watch the Barcelona skyline fade behind you — the open Atlantic ahead.

AGENT'S NOTE

Barcelona in late May is ideal — warm, long evenings, and pre-peak summer crowds. The ETIAS travel authorization (Europe's equivalent of ESTA) is expected to be fully operational by 2027 for US passport holders. Confirm and apply online well in advance. Sagrada Família tickets, particularly tower access, must be reserved months ahead — this is not optional. For the Aura embarkation on May 28, Port of Barcelona is well-served by taxis; allow 45–60 minutes from Eixample hotels during late morning. Have luggage at the port by noon.

CRUISE ITINERARY — MAY 28 – JUNE 10, 2027
Day 1May 28
Embarkation — Barcelona, Spain
Board the Norwegian Aura at Port of Barcelona and settle into your Haven suite — your private sanctuary for the crossing ahead. Explore the brand-new ship: the dramatic Ocean Heights activity complex, Ocean Boulevard promenade, and Haven's private sundeck and pool. Sail away as the sun sets behind Montjuïc.
Day 2May 29
Motril (Granada), Spain
Your first port call — the gateway to Granada and the incomparable Alhambra palace. An NCL excursion to the Alhambra is a rare bucket-list opportunity if you haven't been. Alternatively, explore the white-washed hilltop village of Nerja or simply stroll Motril's Mediterranean waterfront before rejoining the ship.
Day 3May 30
Cádiz (Seville), Spain
One of Europe's oldest continuously inhabited cities, luminous Cádiz sits at the end of a narrow Atlantic peninsula. Consider the full-day excursion to Seville — the Cathedral, the Alcázar, and the winding Barrio Santa Cruz — or explore Cádiz itself: its golden cathedral dome, fresh seafood market, and rooftop terrace views over two seas.
Day 4May 31
Gibraltar, U.K.
The Rock stands guard at the mouth of the Mediterranean — a unique slice of Britain on the Iberian coast. Take the cable car to the top for stunning views across to Africa and back into Europe. Meet the famous Barbary macaques (the only wild primates in Europe), explore St. Michael's Cave, and browse duty-free Main Street below.
Days 5–13Jun 1–9
Nine Days at Sea — The Atlantic Crossing
The heart of the transatlantic experience — long, languorous sea days with nowhere to be. Haven guests enjoy exclusive access to a private pool deck, sundeck, restaurant, lounge, and bar. By day: the Ocean Heights waterslides, ropes course, Mandara Spa treatments, cooking demonstrations, and enrichment lectures. By evening: Broadway-caliber entertainment, specialty dining, and stargazing on the open ocean far from any light pollution.
Day 14Jun 10
Arrival — Miami, Florida
Sail into PortMiami with the downtown skyline ahead and Biscayne Bay sparkling in the morning light. Haven guests receive priority disembarkation. Transfer to your Miami Beach hotel to begin two nights of well-earned Florida sunshine.

MIAMI BEACH ITINERARY — JUNE 10–12
Day 1Jun 10
Arrival & Ocean Drive
Disembark the Aura at PortMiami and taxi over to South Beach. After 13 days at sea, the first afternoon is about reacclimating — lunch on a sidewalk café on Ocean Drive, a slow walk through the Art Deco District, and perhaps a dip in the Atlantic. Dinner tonight at Coya Miami (modern Peruvian, beautiful interiors) or simply the hotel restaurant in flip-flops.
Day 2Jun 11
South Beach & Farewell Dinner
A proper Miami Beach morning — breakfast at Puerto Sagua (legendary Cuban counter), rent bikes on Lincoln Road, and spend the afternoon at the beach. Visit Wynwood Walls for Miami's world-famous street art murals before a standout farewell dinner at Carbone Miami (Italian-American), Zuma (Japanese), or KYU (Asian BBQ) in the Design District — the city's four hottest dinner tables.
Day 3Jun 12
Departure — MIA to DFW
A leisurely hotel checkout and one final Cuban coffee before heading to Miami International Airport (MIA). Allow 45–60 minutes from South Beach hotels. American, Southwest, and United all offer multiple daily direct flights from MIA to DFW.

AGENT'S NOTE

June in Miami is the start of summer — warm, humid, and occasionally showery in the afternoons, but mornings and evenings are perfect. June 10 is also the beginning of Atlantic hurricane season (though storms in early June are historically rare). Restaurants like Carbone and Zuma book out weeks to months in advance, even for walk-ins; advise Mattie and Landry to secure reservations before they depart on the cruise. The Betsy is my top pick for their vibe — it's refined without being stuffy, and the nightly jazz and Ocean Drive location are the most "Miami" experience of the three options at the right price point.
