The New Orleans Casino Primer

Louisiana gaming is a little different than Vegas — worth a quick orientation before you land. There's one land-based casino in the city (Caesars, the old Harrah's, rebranded in 2024) and two riverboat-turned-land casinos a short ride out. All three are 24-hour, all three have full table games and sportsbooks, and all three will happily sign you up for a free players card. Here's how I'd rank them for your trip.
★ Top Pick · Walkable
Caesars New Orleans
8 Canal Street · 12-min walk from hotel
The only land-based casino in Louisiana and the easiest call for your trip — a 12-minute walk from the Bourbon Orleans down Royal Street to Canal. Finished a massive Caesars renovation in 2024, so it's the freshest room in town. Over 1,100 slots and video poker machines, 110 table games, a World Series of Poker room, a high-limit parlor, and a 5,700-sq-ft sportsbook with a 147-foot video wall. The casino is non-smoking inside but has three outdoor smoking/gaming courtyards if that matters to you. Dining includes Nobu, Emeril's Brasserie, and Bobby Flay's Burgers.
Best for: Serious table play, poker, sports betting, late-night energy. Table minimums typically $15–25 on weekends.

Treasure Chest Casino

Things Worth Knowing Before You Play
Video poker is everywhere. Louisiana allows video poker in bars, restaurants, and even truck stops — the French Quarter has little "gaming rooms" tucked into corner bars that'll surprise you. Not glamorous, but perfectly legal and often available when you don't feel like leaving the Quarter.

Sign up for the players card — immediately. At any of the three big casinos, walk up, show ID, and they'll give you a free rewards card on the spot. Even on a short trip it can earn you free play, meal comps, or a room credit. Caesars Rewards and Boyd Rewards both partner with other properties nationwide too.

Bring cash or a debit card. Credit-card cash advances at casino ATMs carry brutal fees (often 7–10%). Debit at the cage or cash on hand is the way. If you want to use one of your new cards to fund the trip, the hotel already being covered helps — you'll mostly need travel cash for meals, rides, and play money.

Louisiana gaming age is 21. They card aggressively; bring your ID every single time.

Smoking policy varies. Caesars is non-smoking indoors (with outdoor gaming courtyards). Boomtown and Treasure Chest allow smoking on most of the floor. If you're smoke-sensitive, Caesars is the call.
